About
Me

> Mathematics Honours Student at The University of Edinburgh

>

As an aspiring mathematician with a focus on algebra, I have a deep passion for exploring the intricacies of abstract mathematical structures. My love for algebra was ignited during my undergraduate studies at the University of Edinburgh, where I took various basic courses in algebra and analysis. Over time, I found myself gravitating towards algebraic topics, particularly the noncommutative kind, inspired by Herstein's Noncommutative Rings.

During my Honours project, I delved into Algebraic Deformation Theory, which further increased my comfort with noncommutative algebra and led me to explore the fascinating world of homological algebra. The strong influence of Hochschild Cohomology on the subject played a significant role in shaping my interests and led me to develop an appreciation for Abelian categories and Homological Algebra. This interest also coincided with my first exposure to algebraic topology and geometry.

My current goal is to develop some understanding of derived functors and categories. I would say my interests are currently:

  • Noncommutative Algebra
  • Algebraic Topology

I am constantly seeking new challenges and opportunities to expand my knowledge in these areas, and I am excited to continue exploring the many fascinating facets of algebra in the years to come.
